[ 1 ] As of 2025, [update] entrepreneur and venture capitalist Peter Thiel [ 2 ] and Italian fashion designer Giorgio Armani are the two richest individuals who identify as either gay, lesbian, bisexual, transgender or transsexual . [ 3 ] History [ edit ] In 1992, record executive, film producer, and media titan David Geffen came out , and is recognized as the first openly gay billionaire in the world. [ 4 ] In 1999, Dolce and Gabbana co-founders and billionaires Domenico Dolce and Stefano Gabbana announced in an interview that they were a gay couple. [ 5 ] Giorgio Armani , one of the richest LGBTQ billionaires In 2000, Jon Stryker , a grandson of Homer Stryker , who founded Stryker Corporation , a medical technologies company, announced that he was gay around the same time as the launch of his Arcus Foundation , one of the largest funders of LGBT charitable organizations. [ 6 ] On August 16, 2013, Jennifer Pritzker made headlines by announcing that she identifies herself as a woman for all business and personal undertakings. This announcement made Pritzker the world's first openly transgender billionaire. [ 7 ] In 2014, Apple CEO and billionaire Tim Cook became the first openly gay chief executive of a Fortune 500 company. [ 8 ] In October 2015, Norway's second richest billionaire Stein Erik Hagen came out as bisexual on the Norwegian talk show Skavlan . [ 9 ] At the 2016 Republican National Convention , billionaire tech investor Peter Thiel announced that he was, "Proud to be Gay". [ 10 ] For most of his career, Giorgio Armani was known for being private about his personal affairs. In 2024, he talked openly about his sexuality and his male and female partners. [ 11 ] In 2024, Forbes estimated that United Therapeutics founder Martine Rothblatt , who is transgender, has a net worth greater than one billion dollars due to the appreciation of her company's stock.
