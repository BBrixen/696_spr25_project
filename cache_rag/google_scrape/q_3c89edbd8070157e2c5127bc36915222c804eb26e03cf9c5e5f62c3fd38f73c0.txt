By Hayley FitzPatrick and Joel Lyons December 9, 2019, 10:00 AM 1:37 Scarlett Johansson, left, and Adam Driver in the film, "Marriage Story." Netflix via AP The nominations for the 77th Annual Golden Globe Awards are in and some of the biggest names in Hollywood are being recognized from the Hollywood Foreign Press Association. Tim Allen, Dakota Fanning and Susan Kelechi Watson presented some of the categories honoring the best in film and television Monday morning. Noah Baumbach's "Marriage Story," starring Adam Driver and Scarlett Johansson, scored six nominations, more than any other film, followed by standouts like "Once Upon a Time ... in Hollywood" and "The Irishman," which both earned five nominations. Hit shows like "The Crown," "Chernobyl" and "Unbelievable" each garnered four nominations. "Barry," "Fleabag," "The Morning Show" and "Big Little Lies" were a few other shows to earn several nods.
