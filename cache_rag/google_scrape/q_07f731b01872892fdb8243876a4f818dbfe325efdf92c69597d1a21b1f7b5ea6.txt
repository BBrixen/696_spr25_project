Members Online • [deleted] Was the AC movie really that bad? // Discussion I know that there was quite a bit of backlash to the 2016 film, but I'm curious as to why it seemingly was so hated? The visuals were stunning, the music was some of the best in the series, and Michael Fassbender fits perfectly as a character in that universe. I feel like it fleshed out Abstergo rather nicely, and gave a fresh look at the universe. That all being said, I get that they should have focused more on the plot of Aguilar, and that they could have payed more attention to continuity (though I'd argue that everything in the film worked with the franchise), and it could have been less...gritty. But it is incredibly difficult to take a story that is usually a 30+ hour interactive experience and force it down into less than 2. And the AC franchise is nearly ten main games deep with several side games and books, comics, etc. And if they had tried to adapt the story of the existing games/characters, I feel like the backlash would have been even stronger. TL;DR: The Assassin's Creed movie wasn't the best, but it was still a stunning visual piece and a worthy add-on to the Assassin's Creed franchise, and I'd appreciate the reasons for why an AC fan wouldn't like it.
