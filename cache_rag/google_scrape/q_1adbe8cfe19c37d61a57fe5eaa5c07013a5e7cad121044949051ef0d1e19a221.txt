Subscribe To Newsletters Oscars 2018: Gary Oldman Wins Best Actor For 'Darkest Hour' By Natalie Robehmed Share Save Consumer Hollywood & Entertainment Oscars 2018: Gary Oldman Wins Best Actor For 'Darkest Hour' By Natalie Robehmed , Former Staff. Mar 04, 2018, 11:24pm EST Share Save This article is more than 7 years old. Gary Oldman won Best Actor for his depiction of Winston Churchill. courtesy Focus Features. As expected, Gary Oldman took home the Academy Award for Best Actor for his performance in Darkest Hour at the annual star-studded event on Sunday night. The actor was nominated alongside Call Me by Your Name 's Timothée Chalamet, Phantom Thread 's Daniel Day-Lewis, Get Out 's Daniel Kaluuya and Roman J. Israel, Esq .'s Denzel Washington. He was odds-on favorite to win the category. "The movies, such is their power, captivated a young man from South London and gave him a dream," said Oldman onstage during his acceptance speech. "Put the kettle on, I'm bringing Oscar home." It is Oldman's first win and second nomination. Jordan Strauss/Invision/AP Oldman, 59, earned the award for his transformation into Winston Churchill in the Universal/Focus Features picture. The Joe Wright-directed period piece follows Churchill as a new Prime Minister torn between negotiating a peace treaty with Nazi Germany, or rallying his nation to fight. The movie grossed $135.8 million worldwide on a $30 million budget and scored six Oscar nominations. It also won the Makeup and Hairstyling category.
