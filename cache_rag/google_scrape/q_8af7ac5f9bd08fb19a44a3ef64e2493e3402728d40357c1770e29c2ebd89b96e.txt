Members Online • sandyWB Top 1% Commenter Disney and the Fox acquisition Industry Analysis A lot of people keep saying that "Disney paid $70 billion for Fox" and that it was a "bad deal" because they didn't earn the money back with the Fox movies released in theaters since then... That's a bit infuriating, and also totally wrong : It didn't cost $70 billion. Disney paid $35.7 billion in cash. The rest was in Disney shares that were newly issued for the occasion. Disney sold Fox Sports to Sinclair for $10 billion . Disney sold Sky to Comcast for $15 billion . Disney sold Endemol Shine Group for $2.2 billion . Disney sold various other Fox assets in the world to comply with antitrust regulations (such as in Mexico and Brazil with sports channels). The deal wasn't just to buy a few upcoming movies in theaters. That's ridiculous. They got all the 20th Century back catalogue (thousands of movies and TV shows like the Simpsons), and also studios and lots, office buildings, TV channels (like FX, National Geographic...), and also 33% of Hulu (which is now worth 15 billion overall).
