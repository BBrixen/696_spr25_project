Donald Trump Donald Trump served as the 45th president of the United States and in 2024, was re-elected to become the 47th president of the nation. Trump was sworn into office as the 47th President of the United States on January 20, 2025. With Trump’s 2024 presidential win, he became the second in history to win non-consecutive terms. The first to do so was Grover Cleveland, the 22nd and 24th president of the United States. During his first run for office, Trump, a real estate developer and reality TV host, was successful in defeating Hillary Clinton for the White House. Trump lost the popular vote, but won the Electoral College, giving him his first presidential win. The 2020 presidential election ended in defeat for Trump. He lost his bid for re-election to the Democratic challenger, President Joe Biden. In 2022, Trump announced that he would be running in the presidential election of 2024, marking the start of his third campaign. In July 2024, Trump announced Ohio Senator JD Vance as his running mate. Issues like the economy and border security were ones the Republicans heavily focused on during their pitch to the American people. Trump was victorious against Vice President Kamala Harris in the 2024 presidential election, winning the popular vote and electoral college to regain his position as POTUS. A Republican, he is known for his unconventional leadership style, focusing on issues like immigration, trade, and deregulation. Before his presidency, Trump was a real estate mogul and the host of "The Apprentice" on NBC. He is running for president again in the 2024 election. Was Donald Trump shot? On July 13, 2024, Donald Trump, former president of the United States and the Republican Party's presumptive nominee for the 2024 presidential election, survived an assassination attempt during a campaign rally near Butler, Pennsylvania. Trump was shot in his upper right ear by Thomas Matthew Crooks, a 20-year-old from Bethel Park, Pennsylvania, who fired eight rounds from an AR-15–style rifle from a nearby rooftop. Then, on September 15, 2024, Trump faced another apparent assassination attempt at his golf course in West Palm Beach, Florida. The suspect, identified as 58-year-old Ryan Wesley Routh, was apprehended after hiding in bushes and aiming a scoped semiautomatic rifle at Trump from approximately 400 yards away, according to FBI officials. Who is Donald Trump’s wife and kids? Donald Trump's wife is Melania Trump, a former model and businesswoman who served as the First Lady of the United States from 2017 to 2021. The couple has one son together, Barron Trump. Trump also has four children from previous marriages: Donald Trump Jr., Ivanka Trump, and Eric Trump with his first wife, Ivana Trump, and Tiffany Trump with his second wife, Marla Maples. How Tall is Donald Trump? Donald Trump is reported to be around 6 feet 3 inches (190 cm) tall, making him notably taller than the average American male. His height has been mentioned in various official records, including medical evaluations released during his time as President. Trump's stature, along with his distinct physical presence, has been a notable aspect of his public image. What is Donald Trump’s Education? Donald Trump began his college education at Fordham University but transferred to the University of Pennsylvania after two years. He graduated from Penn in 1968 with a Bachelor of Science in Economics from the Wharton School of Business. This program, known for its focus on finance and real estate, provided a foundation for his future career in business and real estate.
