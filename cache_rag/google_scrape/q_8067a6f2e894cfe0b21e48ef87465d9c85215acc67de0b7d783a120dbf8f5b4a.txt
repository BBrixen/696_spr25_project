The news as it appeared on Dow Jones: Oct. 13, 2014 2:27 PM EDT: Abigail Johnson Named CEO of Fidelity Investments 2:27 PM EDT: Abigail Johnson Takes Over as Fidelity Investments CEO From Her Father, Ned Johnson 2:27 PM EDT: Ned Johnson to Remain Fidelity Investments Chairman 2:27 PM EDT: Fidelity Reports Management Changes in Internal Memo 2:44 PM EDT: Abigail Johnson Named CEO of Fidelity Investments By Kirsten Grind Fidelity Investments has named Abigail “Abby” Johnson chief executive, according to an internal memo sent by the mutual-fund firm Monday and viewed by The Wall Street Journal. Ms. Johnson, 52, will take over the position from her father, Edward “Ned” Johnson III, who is 84 years old and has been CEO since 1977. Mr. Johnson, whose father founded the company in 1946, will remain chairman, according to the memo. Ms. Johnson holds the position of president of FMR LLC, the parent company of Fidelity Investments, and she will keep that role. The firm, with about $2 trillion of assets under management, is the second-largest mutual-fund firm in the country, after Vanguard Group. “Along with our senior team, we will work together to strengthen Fidelity’s industry leadership and to innovate in ways that enhance the experience we provide to our customers,” Mr. Johnson wrote.
