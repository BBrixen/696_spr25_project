A place where professionals and amateurs alike unite to discuss the field and help each other. Members Online • [deleted] How did James Cameron became so prolific in his 20s? Question I listened to his interview on Howard Stern and he said that he was a truck driver and started learning the craft of film at the age of 24 by going to the library on the weekend and learning everything he could about special effects and so on. He had no film training prior to that but by the time he was 30 he made the Terminator and started filming Aliens. My question is: HOW is that shit even possible? Today we have an ocean of information at our fingertips but seldom pull of the stuff that he's done. There are directors well into their 50s who barely had the success he had.
