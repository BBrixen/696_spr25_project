Members Online • Natties_Garden I'm gaining respect for James Cameron The more time goes by, the more I'm realizing what a remarkable person James Cameron is. With regard to Titanic, he not only weaved an entertaining fictional tale into a well-known historical event, he also directed his film with impeccable attention to detail that ultimately created a masterpiece. More important, I think, is his love for the science. He's spent more time at the actual wreck than most people, even in submarines he designed himself, and he's obsessed with getting the details right. Finally, recent events with the OceanGate sub caused me to gain even more respect for him. He wasn't afraid to call out how unnecessary the Titan disaster was, due to the hubris of its designers (namely Stockton Rush). The contrast between Rush's reckless efforts vs. the painstaking research Cameron has conducted on the Titanic makes me respect his work even more. Read more Archived post.
